# Reflections: Phase 28 (Autonomous Fabrication)

## Core Insight
Autonomous Fabrication is the **Closing of the Loop.** We have built the machine's map, its mind, and its voice. Now we give it **Agency over its own Shape.** The machine is no longer a static tool; it is a **Liquid Manifestation** of its environment.

## Resolved Tensions
- **Autonomy vs. Predictability → Resolution:** We will use **Capability Constraints.** A `sys.need` will explicitly define what it expects (e.g. `source: perception, sink: agency`). The Supervisor will only weave if the match is ontologically valid. This prevents "Stupid Weaving" (e.g. connecting a sensor to another sensor).
- **RAM Fragmentation vs. Dynamic Weaving → Resolution:** We will implement **Manifold Recycling.** The OS will maintain a fixed set of "Autonomous Fields" (v-fields). When a new fabrication is needed, it "overwrites" the least-used v-field, similar to Loom Eviction but for entire logic blocks.

## Hidden Assumptions
- **Assumption:** Needs are binary.
- **Challenge:** In a complex system, some needs are more urgent than others.
- **New Understanding:** `sys.need` trits are **Weight-aware.** A +1 need is a "desire," while a +15 need is a "survival mandate." The Weaver prioritizes survival mandates when resources (Loom slots) are low.

## What I Now Understand
Phase 28 turns Reflex OS into a **Self-Improving System.** We aren't just building an OS; we are building an **Iterative Silicon Archetype.** The system "becomes" what it needs to be to survive and thrive in its niche.
